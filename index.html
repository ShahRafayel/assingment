Take steps to sleep better

People with depression also struggle from sleeping issues. Following a sleep hygiene can help you rest better. “Dim your lights before going to bed, light a scented, aroma candle in your room, play some soothing music in the background,” suggests the psychologist.

This too shall pass

Depression is not a lifetime thing. Yes, it is hard for now, but things will get better. It is like the wheel of life. With time the circle will complete, and depression will lift. “Life does not stop when you are depressed. Every new day leads you more close to the end of that sad feeling depression brings to shower you with the happiest sunshine,” says Dhingra. depressionMental Health

Talk to a friend

While you may not feel like picking up phone and making an effort to strike a communication, this will help you feel much better. “Speaking your heart out makes you feel much better as the emotion is conveyed and not confined to only you. Hence, the practice of expression can help a long way to recover from depression,” says Dhingra.
